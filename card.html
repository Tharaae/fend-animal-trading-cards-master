Golden Retriever

They were named 'retriever' because of their ability to retrieve shot game undamaged.

  • Life Span: 10 - 12 years
  • Weight: 25–34 kg
  • Height: 51–61 cm
  • Colours: Cream, Golden

The Golden Retriever is a large-sized breed of dog bred as gun dogs to retrieve shot waterfowl such as ducks and upland game birds during hunting and shooting parties. Golden Retrievers have an instinctive love of water, and are easy to train to basic or advanced obedience standards.

Labrador

When they were brought to England, they were named after the geographic area known as "the Labrador".

  • Life Span: 12 - 13 years
  • Weight: 25–36 kg
  • Height: 55–62 cm
  • Colours: Yellow, Black, Chocolate

A favourite disability assistance breed in many countries, Labradors are frequently trained to aid the blind, those who have autism, to act as a therapy dog, or to perform screening and detection work for law enforcement and other official agencies. Additionally, they are prized as sporting and hunting dogs.

German Shepherd

The breed was so named due to its original purpose of assisting shepherds in herding and protecting sheep.

  • Life Span: 9 - 13 years
  • Weight: 22–40 kg
  • Height: 55–65 cm
  • Colours: Black & Tan, Black, Grey

The German Shepherd is a breed of medium to large-sized working dog that originated in Germany. The breed's officially recognized name is German Shepherd Dog in the English language. The breed was once known as the Alsatian in Britain and Ireland. It's a relatively new breed, with their origin dating to 1899.

Rottweiler

The buildup to World War I saw a great demand for police dogs, which led to a revival of interest in the Rottweiler.

  • Life Span: 8 - 10 years
  • Weight: 35–60 kg
  • Height: 56–69 cm
  • Colours: Black, Tan, Mahogany

The Rottweiler is a breed of domestic dog, regarded as medium-to-large or large. The dogs were known in German as Rottweiler Metzgerhund, meaning Rottweil butchers' dogs, because their main use was to herd livestock and pull carts laden with butchered meat to market until the mid-19th century.

Poodle

The Poodle is ranked second most intelligent dog breed just behind the Border Collie.

  • Life Span: 12 - 15 years
  • Weight: 20–32 kg
  • Height: 24–60 cm
  • Colours: Apricot, Black, White, Cream

The poodle is a group of formal dog breeds, the Standard Poodle, Miniature Poodle and Toy Poodle. The origins of the poodles are still discussed with a dispute over whether the poodle descends from the old French Barbet breed or from Germany as a type of water dog. It's skillful in many dog sports and activities.
